Chroma Digital

ChromaSim

An empathy simulation lab exploring how digital interfaces can represent diverse cognitive experiences while offering inclusive pivots that support agency, clarity, and access.

Part 1: Strategic & Technical Deep Dive

Each simulation translates lived cognitive experience into frontend behavior, then provides an immediate inclusive pivot that restores agency and access.

Module 01

Dyslexia: The Fluidity of Text

Lived Sensory Reality: Visual tracking fatigue where typography loses fixed stability.

Technical Implementation: A custom useTextDrift hook tracks pointer velocity and applies character-level micro-shifts using Framer Motion across span-split text.

  • Simulation: letter jitter, swaps, kerning shifts, and subtle opacity change on hover/move.
  • Inclusive Pivot: Accessibility Anchor disables motion, applies weighted dyslexia-friendly fonts, increases line height to 2.0, and activates speech with synchronized phrase highlighting.
Open Experience
Module 02

Dysgraphia: Beyond the Keyboard

Lived Sensory Reality: Motor and cognitive friction when non-linear ideas are forced into rigid text inputs.

Technical Implementation: Replace linear text areas with a node-graph canvas via React Flow where users spawn and connect thought nodes.

  • Simulation: multi-modal expression space that values intent over typing precision.
  • Inclusive Pivot: speech-to-text plus semantic smoothing to reduce filler and preserve meaning without erasing voice.
Open Experience
Module 03

Dyscalculia: Visualizing Magnitude

Lived Sensory Reality: Abstract symbols and raw digits can be inaccessible as magnitude indicators.

Technical Implementation: A rendering layer detects and suppresses raw number tokens, replacing them with visual quantity metaphors.

  • Simulation: dynamic block matrices, SVG area charts, and spatial volume representations.
  • Inclusive Pivot: slider-driven gradient expansion and Web Audio pitch shift communicate more/less without numeral dependency.
Open Experience
Module 04

ADHD: Dynamic Pacing & Interest-Driven Flow

Lived Sensory Reality: Peripheral distraction competes with deep focus; engagement increases with immediate feedback.

Technical Implementation: Focus Masking computes active reading bounds and dims all non-active zones with overlay and blur.

  • Simulation: reduced visual competition outside the active task block.
  • Inclusive Pivot: gamified micro-milestones, confetti/haptic feedback, and Save & Return checkpoints to honor pacing shifts.
Open Experience
Module 05

ASD: Predictable, Low-Sensory Sanctuaries

Lived Sensory Reality: sensory intensity and ambiguous language can produce cognitive overload.

Technical Implementation: A global useSensoryEnvironment state manages deterministic layout and literal language rules.

  • Simulation: modular, explicit dashboard with direct step-based copy.
  • Inclusive Pivot: Low-Sensory Mode removes non-essential motion and swaps to muted, earthy tokens for reduced sensory load.
Open Experience
Module 06

Schizophrenia: Grounding and Sensory Salience

Lived Sensory Reality: competing stimuli and altered salience can intensify neutral environmental input.

Technical Implementation: Web Audio spatial channels use PannerNode and focal vectors to vary ambient layers by position.

  • Simulation: layered 3D ambient field that foregrounds gating complexity.
  • Inclusive Pivot: persistent Anchor Tool (stable geometry + metronome pulse) for visual and temporal grounding.
Open Experience
Module 07

Prosopagnosia: Contextual Landscapes

Lived Sensory Reality: face-centric identity systems can fail when facial processing is unreliable.

Technical Implementation: profile cards deprioritize facial imagery and privilege persistent contextual identity metadata.

  • Simulation: color halos, structural icons, role labels, and voice signatures replace face-first recognition.
  • Inclusive Pivot: stable non-facial identity channels remain visible across all collaborative views.
Open Experience
Module 08

Synesthesia: Cross-Wired Sensorium

Lived Sensory Reality: input in one sensory path involuntarily triggers experience in another.

Technical Implementation: keyboard, cursor, and numeric events are mapped to visual blooms, chord generation, and filter modulation.

  • Simulation: keystrokes trigger color ripples; numeric sequences produce procedural harmonics.
  • Inclusive Pivot: users can tune intensity and mapping ranges to maintain exploration without overload.
Open Experience

Part 2: Sign-Up Architecture

The Chroma Hub onboarding form mirrors ChromaSim principles by centering clarity, interdependence, and access from first contact.

Intake Blueprint

Collective Access First

01

About Us & Founder Ethos

Lead with lived-experience leadership and disability justice commitments before data collection.

02

Core Information

Lightweight essentials only: name, pronouns, email, and role pathway with minimal cognitive demand.

03

Engagement Pathways

Clear opt-in tracks for ChromaSim, ChromaShare, ChromaIndex, and ChromaPulse.

04

Interdependence Exchange

Mutual aid prompts: what the applicant can share and what they hope to learn.

05

Sustainability & Access Needs

Capture preferred access supports up front: screen reader, async options, low-sensory mode, and more.

Accessibility Requirements

Baseline standards for all ChromaSim and application workflows.

Keyboard-First Navigation

Sequential focus order with complete form operability through Tab and Enter only.

WCAG AA Contrast

Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for text against all interactive and structural backgrounds.

Screen Reader Semantics

Explicit labels and aria descriptions on all fields, never placeholder-only instruction.

Save & Return Persistence

Auto-save with local storage state checkpoints to prevent cognitive-fatigue data loss.

Part 3: Disability Justice Grounding

ChromaSim is explicitly positioned beyond corporate inclusion language and anchored in systemic transformation.

I

Intersectionality

Design must account for overlapping systems of oppression including ableism, white supremacy, capitalism, and colonialism.

II

Leadership of Those Most Impacted

People who live these realities lead strategy, implementation, and governance decisions.

III

Anti-Capitalist Politic

No interface or policy should measure human worth by productivity output.

IV

Cross-Movement Solidarity

Neuro-inclusion is linked to racial, environmental, queer, and economic justice work.

V

Wholeness, Interdependence, Sustainability

Rest, mutual aid, and collective access are core system requirements, not optional features.
