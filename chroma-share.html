A global network connecting educators who are committed to universal design in the classroom. Teachers share resources, lesson plans, and accessibility strategies — building a living library of inclusive pedagogy that any educator can adapt.

Platform Capabilities

Everything educators need to design, share, and refine accessible learning — in one community-driven hub.

Core

Peer-to-Peer Resource Sharing

Upload lesson plans, slide decks, worksheets, and classroom routines. Every resource can be forked, adapted, and credited back to the educator who shared it.

  • Version history and attribution
  • Tags by subject, age band, and access need
  • Download or remix under open licenses
Templates

Universal Design for Learning

Start from UDL-aligned scaffolds instead of blank documents. Templates encode multiple means of engagement, representation, and expression from day one.

  • Goal + barrier + strategy worksheet
  • Multi-modal lesson plan shells
  • Assessment alternatives built in
Global

Multi-Language Classrooms

Resources surface in the language educators teach in — with community translation layers so inclusive pedagogy travels across borders.

  • Locale-aware search and filtering
  • Community-reviewed translations
  • RTL and script-aware layouts
Audit

Curriculum Accessibility Tools

Run existing materials through guided checks for readability, sensory load, structure, and cognitive accessibility — with actionable fix suggestions.

  • WCAG-oriented document checks
  • Reading level and pacing analysis
  • Exportable remediation reports
Community

Moderated Educator Forums

Ask questions, share wins, and troubleshoot barriers with peers. Discussion spaces are moderated by neurodivergent educators who understand classroom reality.

  • Topic rooms by grade and subject
  • Sensory-friendly async discussion design
  • Clear community guidelines
Integrations

Open-Source EdTech

ChromaShare connects with the tools schools already use — LMS exports, H5P interactives, and open accessibility utilities — without locking you into one vendor.

  • LMS and SIS export formats
  • H5P and OER repository links
  • API hooks for institutional adopters

How ChromaShare Works

Three steps from isolated practice to global collaboration.

1. Share What Works

Upload a resource from your classroom — with context about your learners, constraints, and what you would change next time.

2. Adapt & Audit

Fork a peer's material, run it through accessibility checks, and localize it for your language, culture, and school context.

3. Give Back

Publish your adaptation so the library stays alive. Credit flows to every educator in the chain.
