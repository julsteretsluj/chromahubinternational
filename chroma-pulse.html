Chroma Arts

ChromaPulse

ChromaPulse is a worldwide collective of neurodivergent artists, writers, musicians, filmmakers, and activists. Through exhibitions, performances, publications, and digital campaigns, ChromaPulse makes cognitive diversity visible — challenging stereotypes and celebrating the creative power of different minds.

Collective Capabilities

Platforms, programs, and partnerships that put neurodivergent creators in control of their own stories.

Residencies

Global Fellowships & Residencies

Year-round residency and fellowship programs connect neurodivergent artists with studio space, stipends, mentorship, and time to develop work without masking or rushing.

  • Regional and virtual residency tracks
  • Sensory-accessible studio placements
  • Peer cohorts across disciplines
Festival

Annual ChromaPulse Festival

A hybrid celebration — in-person exhibitions and performances alongside virtual stages — so artists and audiences worldwide can participate on their own terms.

  • Live and streamed programming
  • Captioned, audio-described, and low-stim rooms
  • Open-call submissions from the collective
Archive

Digital Gallery & Media Library

An open-access archive of visual art, film, music, writing, and performance — permanently hosted, properly credited, and free to experience and share.

  • Searchable by medium and theme
  • Creator-controlled licensing options
  • Accessible media formats standard
Campaigns

Collaborative Advocacy

Coordinated digital campaigns across social platforms — art-led messaging that challenges deficit narratives and amplifies neurodivergent voices at scale.

  • Collective campaign briefs
  • Asset kits for allies and partners
  • Impact reporting by reach and engagement
Grants

Micro-Grants for Creators

Small, fast-turnaround grants for neurodivergent creative projects — zines, albums, short films, installations — without bureaucratic gatekeeping.

  • Rolling application windows
  • Flexible budget categories
  • Optional mentor pairing for grantees
Partners

Museum & Gallery Partnerships

Institutional collaborations with museums, galleries, and cultural centers — co-curated shows, touring exhibitions, and permanent collection pathways for neurodivergent artists.

  • Co-curation with lived-experience leads
  • Accessible exhibition design standards
  • Artist fee and rights protections
Workshops

Storytelling for Self-Advocacy

Workshops that teach artists and activists to tell their own stories through visual, written, and performance media — advocacy skills rooted in creative practice.

  • Multi-modal storytelling methods
  • Async and in-person formats
  • Portfolio and pitch development

Creative Disciplines

One collective, many mediums — all united by neurodivergent perspective.

Medium

Visual & Installation

Painting, sculpture, digital art

Gallery exhibitions and public installations that reframe how audiences see cognitive difference — as aesthetic strength, not clinical curiosity.

Medium

Film & Performance

Cinema, theatre, live art

Screenings and stage work with accessible performance design — quiet rooms, content notes, and flexible audience participation built in from the start.

Medium

Writing & Music

Publications, scores, podcasts

Literary journals, recorded albums, and audio storytelling that document lived neurodivergent experience in the artists' own words and sounds.

How ChromaPulse Works

From solo creator to global collective — at your pace.

1. Create & Submit

Share work through open calls, grant applications, or the digital archive. You keep credit and control over how your art is presented.

2. Connect & Collaborate

Join residencies, festival programming, and advocacy campaigns with peers who understand the creative and sensory realities you navigate.

3. Amplify & Advocate

Reach global audiences through institutional partnerships and coordinated campaigns — changing culture, not just galleries.
