About Chroma Hub International

We exist to re-engineer the systems that shape how people learn, work, and participate in civic life — making them universally accessible through psychological insight, open-source EdTech, and data-driven policy.

Our Mission

Chroma Hub International was founded on a simple but radical premise.

To re-engineer global education, workspaces, and civic assemblies into universally accessible environments by leveraging psychological insight, open-source EdTech, and data-driven corporate policy — framing human cognitive diversity not as a deficit, but as a vital spectrum of perspective.

What We Do

Our work operates across three interconnected domains of human experience.

Education

Building accessible classroom networks and advocacy incubators that empower learners of every cognitive profile to thrive.

Workplaces

Advancing neuro-inclusive hiring practices and peer-to-peer transition programs that reshape corporate culture from within.

Civic Life

Creating assemblies and public spaces where diverse minds can participate fully in democratic discourse and community building.

Values

Our work is rooted in disability justice and guided by the communities most impacted by systemic exclusion.

Lived Experience

Those who navigate cognitive diversity and disability firsthand guide our programs, governance, and priorities. Their knowledge is expertise — not anecdote — and it shapes every pillar of our work.

Accountability

We measure our impact transparently, welcome critique from the communities we serve, and hold ourselves responsible when we fall short of the inclusive standards we advocate for.

10 Principles of Disability Justice

Chroma Hub International aligns its mission and practice with these foundational principles of disability justice — a framework for collective liberation.

01

Intersectionality

The framework that explains how various identities and the systems of oppression surrounding them (e.g. ableism, racism, sexism, etc.) can co-exist and interact within a person to create unique experiences of discrimination and/or privilege.

02

Leadership of those most impacted

The principle that the individuals and groups most impacted by systemic oppression should be the ones to lead initiatives and progress in that sector to better represent the spectrum of lived experiences and to achieve true liberation.

03

Anti-capitalist politic

The idea that capitalism disproportionately impacts disabled persons and must be dismantled to achieve true liberation and justice.

04

Commitment to cross-movement organizing

The dedication to aligning with other social justice movements (e.g., racial justice, queer liberation, environmentalism), recognizing that our struggles are tied together and our collective power is stronger.

05

Recognizing wholeness

The importance of seeing all individuals as whole people capable of multitudes regardless of perceived challenges or inabilities.

06

Sustainability

The practice of pacing ourselves and honoring our bodies and minds. It recognizes that rest and recovery are vital components of justice work, requiring us to build sustainability into our organizations and lives rather than pushing past our limits.

07

Commitment to cross-disability solidarity

The commitment to supporting all disabled groups (e.g. physical, chronic, dynamic, mental, and other disabilities) as the impact achieved across all sectors of the disabled community is greater than a single sector.

08

Interdependence

The importance of mutual aid and support especially for those who are multi-marginalized and multiply disabled in order to support the livelihood of all in a society that over values physically demanding and inaccessible labour.

09

Collective access

The principle that access is a collective responsibility, not an individual burden. We move together, and no one is left behind; flexibility and mutual care ensure everyone can fully participate.

10

Collective liberation

The idea that no individual in the disability community is truly included and respected until all disabled people are included, accepted, and treated equitably.
